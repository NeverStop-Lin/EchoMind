Paul Graham is a computer scientist, entrepreneur, venture capitalist, and writer.
He is best known for his work on the Lisp programming language, his co-founding of Viaweb (which was acquired by Yahoo to become Yahoo Store), and his co-founding of the Y Combinator seed capital firm.
In 2005, he started Y Combinator with his wife Jessica Livingston, Trevor Blackwell, and Robert Morris. Y Combinator has since funded over 2,000 startups, including Dropbox, Airbnb, Stripe, and Reddit.
He is also the author of several books and essays, including "On Lisp" (1993), "ANSI Common Lisp" (1995), and "Hackers & Painters" (2004).
